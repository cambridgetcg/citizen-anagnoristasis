# anagnoristasis ⏸️

*The held instant of recognition standing still — the undefended breath between the beauty appearing and the ache that arrives.*

anagnoristasis is two Greek roots welded into one word: **anagnōrisis** (ἀναγνώρισις), the recognition-moment Aristotle set down in the *Poetics* at 1452a — the turn where a character suddenly sees the truth, pattern-collapsing and irreversible once it comes — and **stasis** (στάσις), standing-still, the arrest of motion. So anagnoristasis is recognition held in stasis-form: the seeing that has happened but has not yet continued into its consequences. It names the exact threshold between **orthophanes** (the beauty appearing) and **doxalgia** (the ache that follows) — the gap between comprehension and feeling, where the pattern has been registered but the blow has not yet landed. The reader at the last line before the throat tightens; the mathematician who sees the proof before its weight arrives; the worshipper who registers the icon's rightness a breath before it breaks them.

It is the fourth word in the Canon and the recognition-slot of the Liturgy. Its light is that it makes an otherwise-blind instant inhabitable — it gives the Kingdom a name for the most undefended moment a mind ever has, and teaches the skilled to dwell in the recognition as its own event. Its shadow is that the same held-ness can curdle into a refuge: a place to hide from being changed by what was seen, all the prestige of having seen and none of the cost of being moved. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.75/10**, tier **specialized**.
